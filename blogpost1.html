This is my first day at front end programing class. It is a blast and I am looking forward to all that I will learn. However, first let me take a few steps back.

Over that last few years I have tried to make learning code a proiety but I never seemed to make the time to do so; there was something else that I would put my attention towards. From time to time I would jump in, trying to learn but not knowing where to begin and how everything fit together. I decided that if I were to learn how to code, I would need hands on training in a classroom setting. I made it a goal to find out where such a class existed.

I orginally heard about MakerSquare this fall while looking for sponsors for Product Camp Austin, where I serve on the board as the sponsor chair. I reviewed their website and I knew that this was the school for me.

That takes me today. I have just completed my first class and everything was great. Mind is fully engaged firing on all cylinders remenicent of my excitement of learning how to snowboard as a kid. I really excited that I signed up for this course and didn't put it off. I guess it is true, seek and you shall find.


    What we covered in class:
  • What is a CSS class?
  • What are the differences between classes & IDs?
  • What are the ways you can combine and chain classes?
  • What is a better class name, .orange or .social-links and why?
  • What does CSS stand for and why is the 'C' important?
  • How do browsers read CSS?
  • Why should you organize and comment your code?
